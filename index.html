The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan between 1631 and 1648 for his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It is considered the pinnacle of Mughal architecture.

The Brihadeeswarar Temple in Thanjavur, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a massive 11th-century Dravidian masterpiece dedicated to Lord Shiva, built by Rajaraja Chola I.

Auroville, the "City of Dawn," is an experimental township near Puducherry founded in 1968 as a universal town for human unity beyond religion and politics.

The "Brown Church" represents a spiritual and social movement addressing racial justice rooted in centuries of Latin American Christian heritage.
